Dynos

Examples of dyno charts and what they can tell you about a setup.

What to look for

  • Peak horsepower and peak torque
  • Smoothness of the curve (power delivery)
  • Before vs after on the same dyno and same settings
  • Notes about fuel, temperature, and supporting mods

Tip: dynos are best used for comparison (baseline vs modded), not bragging rights.

Dyno FAQs

Different dyno types, weather correction, tire pressure/strap tension, and gear used can all change results. Compare runs on the same dyno for best accuracy.
The curve often matters more for real driving. A smooth, strong midrange can feel faster than a higher peak with a weak band.
Use the same dyno, same fuel, same tire pressure, and same settings. Do a baseline first, then re-test after the mod with as few variables changed as possible.
